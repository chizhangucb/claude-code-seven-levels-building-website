Level 6 of 7

Data & brand research

A beautiful site that doesn't convert is a Ferrari with no engine. Ground the design and copy in real data — scraped live with Firecrawl.

The technique

Firecrawl scrapes a live site — here apple.com — for brand identity: type scale, palette, spacing, tone of voice. Claude then upgrades the site in accordance. The scrapes themselves are preserved in this repo under process-artifacts/firecrawl/.

This level also produced the best war story of the whole experiment: a concurrent level-7 session silently overwrote Fable's level-6 file. When asked, Fable diagnosed the race, mined the other session's transcript for the exact build recipe, and restored the file — transparently flagging it as a faithful rebuild rather than the original bytes.

The prompts that did it

Scrape the brand, upgrade the site
Hey there. I'd like to go ahead and to apple.com and use my FireCrawl integration. Let's go ahead and grab me the brand identity and then just upgrade this website in accordance with that. Name it as yellow-iphone-web-level-6

Fable scraped apple.com live and applied the identity via a 9.3KB Python patch script with asserts → the “Sunburst” iPhone 17 site. Opus made 39 Firecrawl calls, extracted Apple's type scale, spacing and tone, and restyled accordingly.

Cost & time

What changed vs level 5

  • Copy, specs, and brand feel sharpen once grounded in a live source — no more lorem ipsum energy.
  • The design vocabulary shifts toward the researched brand: dark nav, restrained CTAs, confident headline voice.
  • Concurrent sessions writing to one folder is a race condition — partition outputs or run sequentially.

What this level taught

  • All the data needed to build a site that converts already exists — research it, don't invent it.
  • Session transcripts are recoverable state: an agent rebuilt lost work from another session's log.
