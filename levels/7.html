Design extraction

The strongest lever: extract the full design language of a site you love, and rebuild your product in that language. Translation, not cloning.

The technique

Most people can't articulate why a design is great — 'ample white space' and 'golden ratio' only go so far. A design-extraction skill does it for you: it pulls tokens, typography, easing curves, and load-bearing CSS rules from a target site into a builder brief.

Both runs pointed the skill at antigravity.google. The extraction: grey palette, weight-450 headings on an optical-size ladder, pill shapes, alpha borders, no shadows. Remarkably, both models independently branded their result almost identically — Fable's “Lume” and Opus's “LUMA” — extraction converges.

The skill lives in this repo under skill/design-language/, and Opus's full extraction kit (tokens.json, structure.css, builder brief, verified preview) is under process-artifacts/antigravity-kit/.

The prompts that did it

Extract, then rebuild in that language
Hi there. I'm going to give you a website. I would like to basically understand the design, and I'd like you to follow all the instructions in this file, Namely go to this website, understand the typography, the design, and get it an extraction blueprint so that I can build a website that levels this up. And our website is going to be on selling beautiful phones. @design-language-skill.zip https://antigravity.google/

Both models extracted antigravity.google's design system and rebuilt the phone site in it. Opus produced a full kit directory with a Playwright-verified preview and saved the brand direction to memory.

Push from blueprint to deliverable (Fable follow-up)
I didn't find the website file, where is it? The scaffold only has the framework, not the real website.

The skill's natural output is a kit (brief + tokens + scaffold), not a finished page — budget one more prompt to promote it to the deliverable.

Cost & time

What changed vs level 6

  • Night and day versus level 1 — a coherent, opinionated design identity with real restraint.
  • Both models converged on nearly the same brand name from the same design language.
  • The output is a reusable design system, not just one page.

What this level taught

  • Design extraction gives Claude the vocabulary of a specific great design — the thing humans struggle to articulate.
  • A blueprint is an intermediate artifact; one more prompt turns it into the deliverable.
  • Verify visually at the end — both runs self-checked with screenshots before declaring victory.
