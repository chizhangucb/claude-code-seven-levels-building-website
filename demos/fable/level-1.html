Why yellow?

Because color is a feature. Yellow is the wavelength of optimism — and Apple knows exactly what it's doing when it ships it.

🌞

Instant serotonin

Yellow is the color the human eye notices fastest. Pull it out of your pocket and the whole room gets a little brighter — science-adjacent, vibes-confirmed.

🍋

Aerospace-grade zest

Precision-milled aluminum, color-infused glass, and a finish somewhere between fresh lemon and liquid sunshine. It's a fruit-company phone that finally looks like fruit.

🔍

Never lose it again

Black phones hide in couches. Yellow phones announce themselves from across the room. It's the only iPhone with built-in findability — no AirTag required.

✨

A statement, quietly

Choosing yellow says you don't take yourself too seriously — while carrying one of the most serious pieces of consumer hardware ever made.

A brief history of sunshine

Yellow doesn't show up every year. When it does, it's an event.

2013

iPhone 5c — the original ray

"Unapologetically plastic," gloriously yellow. The 5c was Apple's first yellow iPhone — a candy-shelled rebel that made the whole lineup smile.

2018

iPhone XR — yellow goes big-screen

Yellow returned on a 6.1-inch Liquid Retina display with Face ID. Proof that a flagship-class phone could still be fun.

2019

iPhone 11 — the pastel encore

A softer, mellower yellow paired with dual cameras and Night mode. Arguably the most-loved yellow of them all.

2023

iPhone 14 & 14 Plus — the springtime surprise

Apple dropped a brand-new yellow mid-cycle, in March — a rare move that turned a color release into headline news. Sunshine, on demand.
